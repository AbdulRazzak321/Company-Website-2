Futurice's interview process

Futurice is a software agency. We don’t have our own products and we usually don’t own the software we produce. What Futurice has, is employees. Damn good ones, I might add. We differentiate ourselves from the competition with our people and culture, and recruitment is the place where building a great workplace begins.

If you are applying to Futurice, it’s good to know what we are looking for in future employees. Be your competence in development, design, analytics or business consultancy, we expect the same three qualities from you:

  • You are smart
  • You get things done
  • You fit in our open and transparent culture

All in all, we are looking for a damn nice person.

The two phases of interviews

When you send your application, we evaluate as soon as we can. In some cases the match is very clear, and you might get a straight invite for a meeting. In some cases, we might give you a good old-fashioned phone call where we discuss what kind of opportunities you are looking for and what Futurice has to offer. If we both think that there’s a good fit here, we agree on a proper meeting. Nothing compares to face-to-face discussions.

The first interview is about the culture. If possible, your future supervisor together with some new teammates will be in the interview. We’ll discuss career goals, attitude and people skills. Our people have to get the feeling that they really want to work with you in a real life project. For you, there's a lot of information about Futurice as a company and working place. Have your questions ready, as this is a good palce for you to roast us as well.

The second interview concentrates on your core competence. The interview is all about you. We’ll go through your background, experience and many other aspects important for the day-to-day work. The interview is typically carried out by two current Futurice employees who are more or less specialists in your competence area. You might also get a few exercises to do during the interview.

If you are applying for a more senior role, we might have a third interview. This is always dependent on the case case, and no general rule can be given. Usually it’s about discussing your future role in more detail, or meeting some specific people from our organization.

Calling the shots

At this point, we should both have pretty a good picture of each other. It’s time for decisions. We’ll make a proposal, and then we’ll discuss together on the terms. We’ll introduce our total compensation package, and how our career level model works. Now, it’s up to you to make the final call.

What if we can’t find a suitable position for you in our organization? We like to give feedback to all of our applicants, even if we wouldn’t offer them a position. You have the right to hear why we made the decision we did. We also try to give you some tips and feedback on your CV and application, so you can improve your chances in any future job application.

This is just the outline of our interview process. We hope to see your application, so we can discuss further on Futurice’s culture and the work we do. The chances are that you just might be the future employee we are looking for!
