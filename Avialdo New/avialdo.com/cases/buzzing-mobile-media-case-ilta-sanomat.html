Mobile Solutions

Buzzing mobile media – Case Ilta-Sanomat

The idea: Ilta-Sanomat wants to serve both their readers and advertisers with high quality and easy-to-use contents in all mobile platforms.

What has happened? Long-term cooperation has resulted in renewed mobile sites and launch of mobile versions for IS TV, IS Urheilu & IS Supersää weather service.

The result: Ilta-Sanomat is currently Finland's most read media in the mobile sector, with over ten million weekly visits. Their mobile services have received very good ratings from the readers and the active development work continues.

Ilta-Sanomat accelerating the digital breakthrough

"The media consumers are now mobile. In 2011, the mobile business was still small-scale action, but during the past few years the growth curve has been heading up. And it doesn't look like it'd be slowing down," sums Timo Rinne, Director of Online Services, Ilta-Sanomat.

The boom of mobile devices means the media world has to tackle with fast changing business environment. The biggest efforts to the Ilta-Sanomat mobile were made in 2012. 1,5 years of hard work brought a head start against other Finnish mobile media, and the lead has stayed.

"If it took 10­–15 years from the Internet to change the news field, it took 2–3 years with the mobile", explains Rinne.

"We had a clear vision of what could be done with the mobile, and we believed in it. We've been trying to accelerate the digital breakthrough from our behalf, because we've got more to win than to lose. At the moment, of all the web traffic to the Sanoma Corporation's websites, 52 % is coming from mobile devices."

Mobile full on

We started our common journey with Ilta-Sanomat with a small project, which quickly grew into a long-term and intensive cooperation. Ilta-Sanomat wants to serve their readers despite of time and place, and with the mobile services, that can be easily done.

"Nowadays, you have to publish news fast. Most of our readers look at the news from their mobile phones first thing in the morning, and then finish off their day at our sites in the evening," Rinne says.

In spite of the huge changes in the media field, the financial performance of Ilta-Sanomat grew 1.6 percent in 2013. One of the reasons was the growth of digital business, which offset the losses in print media sector.

"Excellent and innovative mobile services have definitely helped us grow. We are providing our readers a continuous flow of new mobile features, such as rain alerts and news notifications of the most important news of the day."

Advertisers on board!

In addition to the need for fresh and high quality mobile news feed, Ilta-Sanomat also needed to find the best possible advertisers and get them excited about the possibilities of the mobile world.  

In the end of 2011, the first large mobile media campaigns were sold. Success in media advertisement convinced Ilta-Sanomat that the mobile sector could help them to further grow their business. In 2012, the mobile advertisement sales of Ilta-Sanomat doubled.

Telepathic communications & Lean Service Creation

The work isn't over yet. The 10 strong team of Futurice sits in the publishing house of Ilta-Sanomat and develops their mobile services continuously. In the fast-paced media world, Futurice's Lean Service Creation has proved to been the right way of working.

"The method of continuous development and early validation has worked really well. The long-term commitment helps in designing and launching new features, but sometimes software needs to be changed already during the same day. Futurice has been a flexible partner for us," tells Nicklas Koski, Mobile Producer, Ilta-Sanomat.

"I think our communications with Futurice's professionals is getting pretty telepathic; many times we're understanding each other out of one sentence. For us, the most important thing is that our mobile services have the real look and feel of Ilta-Sanomat. Profound understanding of the back-end systems, brand and the right kind of contents of Ilta-Sanomat can only be created over a longer time period," sums Rinne.  

The real look and feel of Ilta-Sanomat

Futurice's Senior Designer Timo Sulanne is the creative force behind the Ilta-Sanomat' apps. Together with his team, he defines how Ilta-Sanomat looks, feels, and evolves in mobile. Ilta-Sanomat is important part of many people's daily lives, and thus the team appreciates all the feedback.

"The fast-paced and ever changing news environment has set lot's of challenges to the work we’ve done. With the amazing reader base of Ilta-Sanomat, the feedback is immediate in everything we do," Sulanne says.

"Feedback based on the broad-scale, almost real-time analytics, has been helping us to validate our design decisions. For me, this has been a fantastic opportunity to be part of Ilta-Sanomat family, and live the mobile growth together with the readers."
